TextSEM: Incorporating Text Data in Structural Equation Modeling

This paper presents TextSEM, an R software package that incorporates textual data into the SEM framework.TextSEM utilizes state-of-the-art NLP techniques (including sentiment analysis, topic modeling, and word embedding) to transform unstructured textual data into quantifiable latent variables, which are then integrated into SEM to analyze the relationships between observed and underlying structures.

Neural Network Analysis of Psychological Data: A Step-by-Step Guide

This paper presents a friendly guide for implementing feed-forward neural networks (FNNs) in psychological research. Using a 3-layer FNN example, we demonstrate core concepts, matrix representation, and optimization. We explore parameter adjustments, structural changes, and introduce methods for result interpretation. Our goal is to help researchers navigate common challenges in applying neural networks to psychological data, bridging theory and practice in machine learning applications.

Advanced Knowledge Tracing: Incorporating Process Data and Curricula Information via an Attention-Based Framework for Accuracy and Interpretability [Preprint]

This paper introduces a Transformer-based framework that links student behaviors to learning outcomes, considering associations between exam and exercise problems. Our approach won first place in the EDM Cup 2023 Contest for predicting exam performance using assignment clickstream data. The framework also offers insights through attention weight analysis, enabling personalized interventions to improve online learning experiences.

Improving mental health support response generation with event-based knowledge graph [Preprint]

This paper addresses the task of generating mental health support responses for online forums, a challenge for existing empathetic conversational systems. To enhance the task, we construct MHKG, a knowledge graph combining eventualities from ASER and contextual relations from Reddit. Experiments show that enriching input with ground-truth neighbors from MHKG significantly improves performance, as confirmed by both automatic and human evaluations.

Comparison of the K1 Rule, Parallel Analysis, and the Bass-Ackward Method on Identifying the Number of Factors in Factor Analysis [DOI]

This study compares three methods for determining the optimal number of factors in factor analysis: the K1 rule, parallel analysis, and the Bass-Ackward method. Through Monte Carlo simulations, we assess their efficacy, including two cessation criteria for the Bass-Ackward method. We provide insights into their performance under various conditions and introduce an online visualization tool for Bass-Ackward factor structures. This research aims to enhance understanding of factor analysis methodology and aid researchers in method selection.
